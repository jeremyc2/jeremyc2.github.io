Fact of the Day!

The Five Pillars of Islam relate very closely to acts of faith in the LDS Church. First, members of both religions are encouraged to perform acts of worship daily to show devotion to God. Modern prophets and Apostles call this our daily bread. It keeps us in communion with God and serves as a reminder of our dependance on him. Knowing this, I was quite satisfied to hear that Muslims take prayer as seriously as Mormons do. For both religions, prayer keeps us in constant remembrance of the one true and living God. Zakat, or the giving of alms to the poor is also fundamental to both peoples. Mormons give Fast Offerings and are encouraged to be good Samaritans in their communities. We are taught that if we pray always and then turn away the poor and the needy, then our prayers do us no good. The Hajj, or pilgrimage to Mecca can easily be compared to temple worship. Those in the United States are often fortunate to go to the temple often, and all Mormons are encouraged to go to the temple as often as circumstances permit, but many save their whole lives to be able to go just once so that they can be sealed in the temple as families and receive the saving ordinances therein. This sacrifice is similar to that of many Muslims who work their whole lives so that they can some day worship in that land considered perhaps just as sacred a place as Mormons see the temple. Muslims may not have concepts of baptism or other saving ordinances, but they do have these comparative ways of being worshipful, generous, and kind. Just like our rituals, these small and simple acts keep them active in their faith in God in a world that evermore denies the existence of a Supreme Being.
